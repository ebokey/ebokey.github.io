UBC Supermileage Team

The UBC Supermileage Team is a student-led team that designs and builds three super-efficient vehicles that compete in international competitions:

  • A Prototype class vehicle with a gasoline internal combustion engine
  • A battery-electric Urban Concept class vehicle
  • A hydrogen fuel-cell Prototype vehicle
Through this work the team raises awareness for sustainable transportation, and also provides students with invaluable learning experiences.

Roles

Team Captain

September 2021 - August 2023
  • Managed a 51-member student team that designs and builds three vehicles for international energy-efficiency competitions
  • Oversaw a yearly team budget of around $75,000, and managed engineering resources to build and optimize the team vehicles
  • Coordinated and represented the team at events such as the UBC APSC Open House and Energy Disruptors Unite to raise awareness of STEM and the team, and network with sponsors
  • Led the team to a second-place finish at the 2022 SAE Supermileage Competition with a mileage of 1038 miles per US gallon, and a third-place finish at the 2023 Competition Supermileage de Quebec

Vehicle Mechanics Division Lead

September 2019 - August 2021
  • Managed 20+ division members, and helped with the completion of over 15 technical projects such as the design of a composite roll bar and vacuum infusion research
  • Oversaw the design, manufacturing, and testing of the team's first hydrogen fuel cell vehicle
  • Developed training programs for various software packages (e.g. SolidWorks, ANSYS, Star-CCM+) to teach new members useful design tools
  • Designed a new composite chassis using ANSYS ACP that reduced the chassis mass by 28%
  • Led the research and development of a new aerodynamic body using STAR-CCM+ that showed a potential drag reduction of 25%

GrabCAD Manager

September 2019 - August 2021
  • Managed the team's GrabCAD system, which is used for the storage and revision control of CAD files
  • Documented and maintained naming standards, folder structure, and best practices to help improve the team's CAD creation and management
  • Introduced and familiarized team members with the system so that they can create 3D designs collaboratively

Vehicle Mechanics Division Member

September 2018 - August 2019
  • Redesigned the hydraulic braking system for the team's Urban Concept vehicle
  • Helped with the construction of a new chassis for the Urban Concept vehicle
  • Assisted with the construction of a new external shell for the team's Gasoline Prototype vehicle
  • Resolved vehicle issues at both the Shell Eco-Marathon Americas and the SAE Supermileage competition, and helped the team achieve 2nd place at both competitions as well as a mileage of 2229 miles per US gallon (the second-best in team history)

Gallery

Gasoline Prototype

The gasoline Prototype at the Shell Eco-marathon Americas 2019 competition. A lot of work had gone into this vehicle over the course of the year, including multiple engine modifications and weight-reduction on almost all components. This work paid off, as the vehicle placed 2nd with 1372 mpg (and was less than 200 mpg off of 1st place).

Urban Concept

The team testing our battery electric Urban Concept in February 2022 by taking a stroll around campus. Despite the underwhelming testing location, this was a big moment for the team as the Urban Concept vehicle had not run for such an extended period of time as a battery electric vehicle before.

Fuel Cell Prototype

The fuel cell Prototype running at Mission Raceway in July 2021. This was the first time the vehicle was tested, as it was built from the ground-up over the summer of 2021. While there were some issues discovered with the vehicle, the testing was successful in verifying the vehicle works, and that it has very high efficiency potential.

Mission Raceway 2021

Team members during the test of the fuel cell Prototype at Mission Raceway in July 2021. The 'track day' at Mission Raceway was a big moment for the team. While not quite the same as competition, it was a great opportunity for members who hadn't been to competition before to get some exposure to a similar environment. There was a lot of pressure to get the new fuel cell vehicle running, but everyone performed exceptionally and we finally got to see all our hard work validated when the vehicle successfully ran.

SEMA 2019

Team members, pictured with the gasoline Prototype and battery electric Urban Concept, at the Shell Eco-marathon Americas 2019 competition. This competition marked the end of an era in a sense, with so many graduating members that year. We were able to end the era with a bang, placing 2nd with the gasoline Prototype. The Urban Concept vehicle passed technical inspection, which was a big accomplishment as the team had just converted it to battery electric on very short notice.

SAE 2019

Team members, pictured with the gasoline Prototype, celebrating a 2nd place finish at the SAE Supermileage 2019 competition. This competition was another high note to end an era on as we reached 2229 mpg, the second best result in team history.
